2. WHAT WAS WRONG IN RC-133
================================================================================

RC-133 tested whether physical permutations (P23, P11, S) preserve the 12D code
subspace of F2^24. They do not. This led to the conclusion that the dynamics are
"not unitary on the code space."

THE ERROR: RC-133 confused the code space (a 12D subspace of F2^24) with the
logical space (a 12D subspace of the 48D symplectic space). In the stabilizer
formalism:

  • Physical space: 24D (coordinates 0–23)
  • Symplectic space: 48D (X part + Z part)
  • Stabilizer group: 12-dimensional
  • Logical space: 12-dimensional (24 logical Pauli operators modulo stabilizers)

Physical permutations act on the symplectic space by permuting both X and Z
parts simultaneously. They preserve the stabilizer group as a set (since they are
automorphisms of the code), but they do not preserve the code subspace as a
vector space because they mix the code with its dual.

THE CORRECTION: Test unitarity in the 48D symplectic space, where the symplectic
form is the quantum inner product.
