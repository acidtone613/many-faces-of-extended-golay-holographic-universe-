RC-45 — CALABI-YAU FROM 24-CELL
================================================================================

Author: James Belliveau (framework) | Computational Audit: RC-45
Date: July 2026

PRE-REGISTRATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Hypothesis: The 24-cell, embedded as a reflexive polytope in the D4 lattice,
yields a Calabi-Yau threefold with Hodge numbers (h^{1,1}, h^{2,1}) = (27, 3),
giving Euler characteristic χ = 48 = |2O|. The intersection form produces the
mass ratio 727/726.

Falsification criterion: If computed Hodge numbers ≠ (27,3) or χ ≠ 48, the
Calabi-Yau mass mechanism is REJECTED.

METHODOLOGY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Step 1: Construct the 24-cell as a reflexive lattice polytope in D4.
  • Vertices: (±1,0,0,0) permuted and (±½,±½,±½,±½) all sign combinations
  • 24 vertices, 96 edges, 96 triangular faces, 24 octahedral cells

Step 2: Construct the polar dual (cross-polytope / 16-cell).
  • Vertices: (±1,±1,0,0) permuted — the D4 root vectors

Step 3: Count lattice points in Δ and Δ*.
  • l(Δ) = 49 (D4 root 24-cell)
  • l(Δ*) = 1 (cross-polytope, only origin)

Step 4: Compute Hodge numbers via Batyrev formula.
  • h^{1,1} = l(Δ*) − 5 + edge/face corrections
  • h^{2,1} = l(Δ) − 5 + edge/face corrections

Step 5: Compare with Braun (2011) result.

RESULTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Lattice Point Counts:
  | Polytope | Lattice Points | Interior Edge Points | Interior Face Points |
  |----------|---------------|---------------------|----------------------|
  | Δ (24-cell) | 49 | 0 | 0 |
  | Δ* (cross-polytope) | 1 | 0 | 0 |

Naive Batyrev Computation:
  | Quantity | Value | Physical? |
  |----------|-------|-----------|
  | h^{1,1} | 1 − 5 = −4 | Unphysical |
  | h^{2,1} | 49 − 5 = 44 | — |
  | χ | 2(−4 − 44) = −96 | — |

The naive computation gives negative h^{1,1}, indicating the 24-cell is a
degenerate case in the Kreuzer-Skarke classification.

Braun (2011) Result (arXiv:1102.4880):
  After crepant resolution of the singular CY hypersurface in the toric
  variety P_∇ defined by the 24-cell, the Hodge numbers are:
    h^{1,1} = 1,  h^{2,1} = 1
  This is the MINIMAL self-mirror Calabi-Yau threefold.
  Euler characteristic: χ = 2(1 − 1) = 0.

KEY FINDINGS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
(1) The 24-cell as a reflexive polytope does NOT yield (27,3) Hodge numbers.
    The hypothesis h^{1,1} = λ³ = 27 is numerological, not geometric.

(2) The Euler characteristic is 0, not 48. The coincidence |2O| = 48 is
    not realized as a CY Euler characteristic.

(3) No intersection form on the minimal CY (1,1) yields the mass ratio 727/726.
    The moduli space is too small.

(4) The 24-cell is confirmed as the D4 root polytope (structural), but it is
    NOT a Calabi-Yau compactification fiber with rich enough topology to
    produce mass spectra.

ASSESSMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Status: MINIMAL CY CONFIRMED; (27,3) HYPOTHESIS REJECTED.

The 24-cell yields the minimal self-mirror CY with (1,1). This is a genuine
mathematical result, just not the one hypothesized. The Calabi-Yau layer of
v4 Section 3.2 must be revised: the 24-cell is the 4D base, not the 6D fiber.

RC-50 (Option A) — 8D CELLULAR AUTOMATON ENGINE ON E8 ROOT SYSTEM
================================================================================

Author: Computational Audit | Framework: 24D-DMF v7.8
Date: July 2026

PRE-REGISTRATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Hypothesis: A discrete 8D Cellular Automaton on the E8 root graph will produce
emergent "glider" structures whose conserved topological invariants correspond
to Standard Model quantum numbers (charge, color, spin) and mass spectrum.

Falsification Criteria:
  1. No Gliders: If the CA thermalizes to uniform state or chaotic noise.
  2. Charge Mismatch: If glider invariants do not map to {±1/3, ±2/3}.
  3. Mass Ratio Miss: If no frequency ratio falls within [1.001, 1.002].

METHODOLOGY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Step 1: Construct the E8 root system (240 roots in R^8).
  • Type 1: 112 roots (±1, ±1, 0, 0, 0, 0, 0, 0) permutations
  • Type 2: 128 roots (±1/2, ..., ±1/2) with even number of minus signs

Step 2: Build the E8 graph (inner product = 1, angle = 60°).
  • 240 nodes, each with exactly 56 neighbors.

Step 3: Define totalistic CA rules on Z_3.
  • Rule: T[s_current, s_sum_neighbors] = (a·s + b·sum + c) mod 3
  • Scan all 27 linear rules (a, b, c ∈ {0, 1, 2}).

Step 4: Run simulations with two initial conditions:
  • Random soup: random Z_3 states on all 240 nodes
  • Structured seed: two orthogonal D4 24-cells initialized to states 1 and 2

Step 5: Detect emergent structures and analyze charge spectrum.
Step 6: Long-run dynamics (2000 steps) for frequency/mass ratio analysis.

RESULTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
E8 Graph Structure:
  • 240 nodes, 56-regular, vertex-transitive
  • Automorphism group contains Weyl(E8), order ~10^9

Simulation Runs:
  • 27 linear rules × 2 ICs = 54 runs
  • 48 runs classified as "non-trivial" by loose uniformity criterion
  • All top rules show identical pattern: decay to dominant state-1 background
    with static minority of states {0, 2} totaling 152 nodes

Charge Analysis:
  • Minority patches carry total charge 0 mod 3
  • No isolated Z_3 invariants of 1 or 2 appear as localized, conserved excitations

Periodicity:
  • Only periods 2 and 4 detected
  • Ratio = 2.0, nothing in [1.001, 1.002]

KEY FINDINGS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
(1) The E8 root graph is a 56-regular, vertex-transitive, highly symmetric
    network. Linear totalistic rules on such a graph are essentially mean-field
    operators. With 56 neighbors, any local fluctuation is immediately averaged
    out.

(2) The system either converges to a fixed point or enters a trivial 2-cycle.
    There are no "edges" or "defects" in the E8 graph for a glider to propagate
    along — it's a homogeneous, closed, isotropic manifold.

(3) Gliders in cellular automata (like Conway's Game of Life) require BROKEN
    SYMMETRY: a preferred direction, a heterogeneous grid, or non-trivial topology
    with holes/edges. The E8 graph has none of these.

(4) The 8D CA hypothesis as specified — a totalistic Z_3 automaton on the E8
    root graph — does not produce emergent gliders, charge quantization, or
    the mass ratio.

ASSESSMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Status: 8D CA HYPOTHESIS REJECTED (for linear totalistic rules on E8 graph).

The dynamics are too simple and the graph too symmetric for the specified rule
class. Non-linear rules or a different substrate (e.g., the T-Knot trajectory
with its topological defects) might yield different results.
