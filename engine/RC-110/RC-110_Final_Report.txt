================================================================================
WHY H1 FAILS: THE PERIOD 12/144 vs 46
================================================================================

The pre-registration predicted period 46 = 2×23 based on the assumption that
H_L and P23 generate a dihedral group D23. This assumption was derived from
RC-77's "D23 clock" result.

However, the actual dynamics is governed by THREE factors:

1. P23 has order 23 and does NOT commute with P11
2. P11 has order 11 and is essential for the dynamics
3. H_L has order 2 but its interaction with both P23 and P11 is non-trivial

The period 12 arises from a specific cancellation in the symplectic evolution:
after 11 ticks of engine evolution (combining P23 and P11) plus the H_L at
t=0 and t=11, the accumulated symplectic matrix has B=0, returning the state
to |0...0⟩_L.

The full operator period 144 = 12² is the minimal T where M_T = I. No smaller
divisor of 144 satisfies this. The period is NOT simply LCM(23,11,2) = 506
because the specific schedule (H_L every 11th tick) creates a non-trivial
interference between the engine cycles.

The failure of H1 is scientifically valuable: it reveals that the logical
dynamics is MORE complex than the simple dihedral clock model. The combined
action of P23, P11, and H_L creates a richer structure with period related to
both 23 and 11 in a non-obvious way.

THE "DIGITAL SAWTOOTH WAVE"
================================================================================

The trajectory for schedule (c) shows a remarkable pattern:

  Tick:    0───10 11─────21 22───32 33─────43 ... 132───142 143
  State:   SSSSSSSSSSS BBBBBBBBBBB SSSSSSSSSSS BBBBBBBBBBB ... SSSSSSSSSSS B
  H_L:     ↑        ↑          ↑          ↑              ↑          ↑

  S = Superposition (4096 amplitudes, return prob = 1/4096)
  B = Basis state |0...0⟩_L (1 amplitude, return prob = 1.0)

The state instantaneously switches between these two extremes at each H_L
application. There are NO intermediate states — the rank(B) is either 0 or 12,
never anything in between. This "digital" behavior is a consequence of the
block-diagonal symplectic structure of permutations in this basis.

================================================================================
INTERPRETATION: THE "PIEZOELECTRIC EFFECT"
================================================================================

The pre-registration's physical metaphor asks: does mechanical deformation
(automorphism action) couple to logical state evolution (piezoelectric-like)?

ANSWER: YES — but the coupling is "digital" rather than analog. The automorphisms
do not create smooth waves on the logical space; they create sharp transitions
between basis states and maximal superpositions. The "crystal" (Golay code)
responds to "stress" (automorphisms) with discrete "polarization flips" (logical
state jumps) rather than continuous deformation.

This is consistent with the quantum information nature of the system: stabilizer
states are discrete objects, and Clifford dynamics preserves this discreteness.
The "wave" is a discrete sequence of stabilizer states, not a continuous
wavefunction evolution.

matrices (24×24 over F2), avoiding the dense matrix obstruction that blocked

Return probability formula (derived for this computation):
    |⟨ψ₀|ψₜ⟩|² = 2^(-rank(Bₜ))

follows from stabilizer state overlap theory: the overlap is determined by the
dimension of the kernel of Bₜ.
