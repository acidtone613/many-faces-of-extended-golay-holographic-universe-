1. PRE-REGISTERED HYPOTHESIS AND FALSIFICATION CRITERIA
================================================================================

1.1 HYPOTHESIS (from v8.05 §3.2.1)
-----------------------------------

In a symmetry-preserving barycentric triangulation of the 24-cell, the
Dirac operator has a degenerate eigenspace at eigenvalue λ* containing
modes that project onto the 7D and 9D⁻ tunnel subspaces. The lexicographic
triangulation breaks this symmetry, lifting the degeneracy by δ where
2δ/λ* = 1/726.

1.2 PRE-REGISTERED FALSIFICATION CRITERIA
------------------------------------------

Criterion A: No 2D degenerate eigenspace exists in the low-energy
             edge-face subspace → CONJECTURE REJECTED

Criterion B: A 2D degeneracy exists but eigenvectors have zero projection
             onto 7D/9D⁻ tunnel subspaces → CONJECTURE REJECTED

Criterion C: A 2D degeneracy with tunnel projection exists but at eigenvalue
             incompatible with the 727/726 ratio mechanism → CONJECTURE REVISED

3. WHAT WAS NOT COMPUTED
================================================================================

3.1 FULL SPECTRUM SEARCH
-------------------------
    The Dirac operator has 5,088 eigenvalues. Only 600 were computed
    (~12% of the spectrum, covering |λ| ≤ 1.37).

    A 2D degeneracy satisfying the conjecture could exist at
    |λ| > 1.37. The spectral radius is bounded by the maximum
    vertex degree in the barycentric 1-skeleton, likely < 10.

    WHY NOT: eigsh with k=600 already took ~97 seconds. Searching
    the full spectrum (k=2,544 for positive eigenvalues) would
    require shift-invert methods or multiple spectral windows,
    with total compute time potentially hours.

3.2 W(F4) SYMMETRY EQUIVARIANCE TEST
-------------------------------------
    The conjecture assumes W(F4) commutes with D. This was not
    tested by explicit group action computation.

    WHY NOT: Constructing all 1,152 W(F4) elements as 4×4 matrices
    and their induced action on the 5,088-dimensional chain complex
    is combinatorially intensive. The observed multiplicities
    (4, 6, 8, 9, 12, 16, 17, 18, 22) are not pure W(F4) dimensions
    ({1, 2, 4, 6, 8, 9, 12, 16}), suggesting either:
        (a) Accidental degeneracies, or
        (b) A larger symmetry group than W(F4)

    Distinguishing (a) from (b) requires the explicit equivariance test.

3.3 PROPER TUNNEL SUBSPACE DEFINITION
--------------------------------------
    The MOG "7D/9D⁻ tunnel" is not merely two vertices. In v8.05
    (RC-61), it is defined by:
        — 7D position (index 6 in MOG)
        — 9D⁻ position (index 13 in MOG)
        — Path: 8D → 9D⁻ → 6D with Manhattan distance 6 = Gram gap
        — Inclusion-exclusion count yielding mode index 173

    In the barycentric complex, the "tunnel subspace" should include:
        — Original vertices 6 and 13
        — Edge-midpoints on paths between them in the 24-cell graph
        — Face-centroids of faces containing these edges
        — Cell-centroids of cells containing these faces

    This is a GEOMETRIC subspace, not just two basis vectors.
    I projected onto only the two vertex basis vectors (6, 13).

    WHY NOT: Defining the full path subspace requires graph-geodesic
    computation on the 24-cell vertex graph and its barycentric lift.
    This was not implemented.

3.4 EIGENVALUE RATIO TEST
--------------------------
    Even if a 2D degeneracy with tunnel projection were found, the
    conjecture requires that symmetry-breaking yields the specific
    ratio 727/726. This requires:
        — Identifying the symmetry-breaking perturbation V
        — Computing its restriction to the 2D degenerate subspace
        — Verifying the eigenvalue splitting gives 2δ/λ* = 1/726

    WHY NOT: No 2D candidate subspace was found, so this test
    could not be initiated.

5. ANALYSIS AND INTERPRETATION
================================================================================

5.1 PARTIAL NEGATIVE EVIDENCE
------------------------------
    The search of the lowest 600 eigenvalues (~24% of the positive
    spectrum, ~12% of the full spectrum) found:
        — ZERO 2D degeneracies in the edge-face subspace
        — ZERO 2D degeneracies with non-zero tunnel projection
        — The only 2D non-zero degeneracy is in the wrong subspace
          (face-cell, not edge-face)

    This is strong negative evidence. The conjecture requires a
    very specific spectral feature (2D + edge-face + tunnel), and
    this feature is absent from the low-energy regime where physical
    modes would naturally reside.

5.2 STRUCTURAL CONCERN
-----------------------
    The barycentric subdivision and the lexicographic triangulation
    are DIFFERENT simplicial complexes:
        — Barycentric: 1,152 tetrahedra, full W(F4) symmetry
        — Lexicographic: 96 tetrahedra, broken symmetry (Z₂×Z₂ stabilizer)

    There is NO canonical map between their Dirac spectra. The
    eigenvalue scales are different, the mode structures are different,
    and the "modes 173/174" of the lexicographic complex have no
    direct counterpart in the barycentric complex.

    The conjecture assumes a correspondence that is not mathematically
    guaranteed. This is a fundamental structural weakness independent
    of any computational result.

5.3 THEORETICAL OBSTRUCTION
----------------------------
    The 1-form Laplacian L₁ = d₁d₁ᵀ + d₂ᵀd₂ is not a W(F4)
    representation in the naive sense. The 1-form space C¹ of the
    barycentric subdivision decomposes as:
        C¹ ≅ (C⁰_vertices ⊗ C¹_edges) ⊕ (C¹_edges ⊗ C²_faces)

    This is a tensor product structure, not a direct sum of W(F4)
    irreps. The Hodge Laplacian couples these tensor factors through
    the incidence maps d₁ and d₂.

    Therefore, even if W(F4) acts on the barycentric complex, the
    Laplacian eigenvalues need not organize into pure W(F4) dimensions.
    The observed "non-W(F4)" multiplicities (17, 18, 22) may simply
    reflect this tensor product structure.

6. WHAT WOULD BE REQUIRED FOR A COMPLETE TEST
================================================================================

6.1 FULL SPECTRUM COMPUTATION
-------------------------------
    REQUIRED: Compute all 5,088 eigenvalues of D, or at minimum all
    positive eigenvalues (2,544 values).

    METHOD: Shift-invert spectral transformation. The spectrum is
    symmetric, so only positive eigenvalues need be computed.
    Use scipy.sparse.linalg.eigsh with sigma=0 (shift-invert) to
    find eigenvalues near a target, iterating across spectral windows.

    ESTIMATED TIME: 2–6 hours depending on hardware and convergence.

    ALTERNATIVE: Compute D² (Hodge Laplacian on full complex) and
    find its eigenvalues. D² is block-diagonal with blocks L₀, L₁,
    L₂, L₃. The positive eigenvalues of D are the square roots of
    the positive eigenvalues of D². This may be more efficient.

6.2 EXPLICIT W(F4) EQUIVARIANCE TEST
-------------------------------------
    REQUIRED: Construct the 1,152 elements of W(F4) as 4×4 orthogonal
    matrices, compute their induced action on the barycentric chain
    complex, and verify [D, g] = 0 for all g ∈ W(F4).

    METHOD:
        — Generate W(F4) from its Coxeter generators (simple reflections)
        — For each group element g, construct the permutation matrix
          acting on barycentric simplices
        — Verify D P_g = P_g D (or compute ||D P_g − P_g D||)

    ESTIMATED TIME: 1–2 hours for group generation and testing.

    PURPOSE: Distinguish accidental degeneracy from symmetry-forced
    degeneracy. If [D, g] = 0, then degeneracies MUST be sums of
    W(F4) dimensions. If not, degeneracies are operator-specific.

6.3 PROPER TUNNEL SUBSPACE DEFINITION
--------------------------------------
    REQUIRED: Define the "7D/9D⁻ tunnel" as a subspace of the
    barycentric chain complex, not just two vertex basis vectors.

    METHOD:
        — In the 24-cell vertex graph, find all shortest paths
          between vertex 6 and vertex 13
        — For each path, include the corresponding edge-midpoints,
          face-centroids, and cell-centroids in the barycentric complex
        — The tunnel subspace is the span of all these basis vectors
          (potentially 50–200 dimensions)
        — Project eigenvectors onto this subspace and test for
          significant overlap

    ESTIMATED TIME: 30 minutes for graph computation and subspace
    construction.

6.4 EIGENVALUE RATIO TEST (CONTINGENT)
--------------------------------------
    REQUIRED ONLY IF 6.1–6.3 yield a candidate 2D degeneracy:

    — Identify the symmetry-breaking perturbation V that maps the
      barycentric triangulation to the lexicographic triangulation
    — Restrict V to the 2D degenerate subspace
    — Compute the eigenvalue splitting and verify 2δ/λ* = 1/726

    NOTE: This step is ill-defined because the two triangulations
    are different complexes, not two perturbations of the same complex.
    There is no natural perturbation V connecting them.
