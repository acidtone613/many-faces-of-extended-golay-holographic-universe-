KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. The 5 colors form an ordered spectrum from "variable/weak" to "uniform/strong".

2. Blue (Weakest, highest variance) → Weak Force:
   - The weak force is weak because its mean shattering amplitude is lowest.
   - Its high variance corresponds to the W/Z mass splitting (spontaneous
     symmetry breaking). One state is active (W/Z, massive), one is dormant
     (photon, massless) — this is the electroweak unification.

3. Green (Weak, zero variance) → QED:
   - The photon has no self-interaction, no mass, no variance.
   - Green contains the lightest massive state (electron) from RC-153b.

4. Yellow (Strong, zero variance) → QCD:
   - Gluons are massless but self-interacting.
   - The zero variance reflects the exact SU(3) color symmetry.
   - The strong amplitude reflects confinement.

5. Red (Middle, moderate variance) → Higgs:
   - The Higgs doublet has a non-zero vacuum expectation value (v) and a
     zero mode. The moderate variance captures this doublet structure.
   - Red sits between the weak and strong forces — it couples to both.

6. Orange (Strongest, low variance) → Gravity / Framework Boundary:
   - Not a gauge force. The strongest amplitude reflects the curvature of
     the talychon itself.
   - Low variance = no screening, no charge, always attractive.

7. Each color has exactly 2 states. This is the framework's "doublet" structure.
   It could represent:
   - SU(2) weak doublets (Blue)
   - Particle/antiparticle pairs (Green, Yellow)
   - Higgs doublet (Red)
   - Framework boundary states (Orange)
