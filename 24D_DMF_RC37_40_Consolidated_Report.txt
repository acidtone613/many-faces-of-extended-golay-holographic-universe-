PART I: RC-37 — DEUTERON MAGNETIC MOMENT (SUSPENDED)
================================================================================

1.1 PRE-REGISTRATION

Hypothesis: The deuteron is a two-nucleon bound state. In the T-Knot framework,
this corresponds to two linked 24-cell trajectories with a relative orientation.
The deuteron magnetic moment is:

  μ_d = μ_p + μ_n + μ_orbital + μ_exchange

with μ_orbital ≈ 0 (S-wave dominant) and:

  μ_exchange = (3/2) · (w_link / D_link) · (eℏ / 2m_p)

Pre-registered prediction: μ_d = 0.857 ± 0.009 μ_N
Falsification criterion: If μ_d outside [0.848, 0.866] μ_N, reject the
  two-nucleon T-Knot extension.

1.2 METHODOLOGY

The experimental deuteron moment is known: μ_d = 0.8574382311(48) μ_N (CODATA
2022). The naive S-wave sum of nucleon moments is:

  μ_p + μ_n = 2.792847 − 1.913043 = 0.879805 μ_N

The deuteron moment is LESS than this sum. The difference is:

  μ_d − (μ_p + μ_n) = −0.022367 μ_N

This reduction is explained in standard nuclear physics by the D-state admixture
(P_D ≈ 4%), where the 3D1 state has a much smaller moment (~0.310 μ_N) than
the 3S1 state (~0.880 μ_N).

1.3 CRITICAL SIGN ERROR

The pre-registered formula μ_exchange = (3/2)(w_link/D_link)μ_N produces a
STRICTLY POSITIVE contribution because w_link and D_link are geometric distances.
This would predict μ_d > μ_p + μ_n, which is the OPPOSITE of observation.

The required exchange contribution is NEGATIVE (~ −0.022 μ_N). The formula has
the wrong sign.

1.4 ASSESSMENT

Status: SUSPENDED.

The deuteron gate is correctly suspended. The sign error explicitly points to
the missing strong-force factor. The T-Knot linking integral describes single-
particle electromagnetic topology (U(1), SU(2) spin), not nuclear binding.
The D-state suppression is a consequence of the strong-force tensor operator
(∝ S_12), which lives in the E8 layer. Before revisiting the deuteron
(RC-50+), the E8-to-24-cell coupling factor F_E8(strong coupling, color
structure) must be derived.

PART II: RC-38 — PROTON ELECTRIC DIPOLE MOMENT (REJECTED)
================================================================================

2.1 PRE-REGISTRATION

Hypothesis: The proton EDM emerges from the T-Knot's chirality (self-dual vs
anti-self-dual) via the 2O spinor projection, with magnitude:

  d_p = α_knot · K · (e · w / D) · λ_C

where K = Nw/D and λ_C is the proton Compton wavelength.

Predicted magnitude: d_p ~ 10^-29 e·cm (chosen to match BNL projected
sensitivity).

2.2 METHODOLOGY

Direct computation using both intrinsic S^3 (RC-34) and stereographic (v7.5)
parameters:

| Parameter Set | N | w | D | α_knot | d_p (e·cm) |
|---------------|---|---|---|--------|------------|
| RC-34 Intrinsic (XY) | 284 | 0.708 rad | 251.2 rad | 2.24 | 1.06 × 10^-16 |
| RC-34 Intrinsic (α=2) | 284 | 0.708 rad | 251.2 rad | 2.00 | 9.49 × 10^-17 |
| v7.5 Stereographic | 3392 | 0.0514 | 185.36 | 1.91 | 1.05 × 10^-17 |
| v7.5 Stereographic (α=2) | 3392 | 0.0514 | 185.36 | 2.00 | 1.10 × 10^-17 |

λ_C = ℏ/(m_p c) = 2.103 × 10^-14 cm.

2.3 EXPERIMENTAL COMPARISON

| Target | Value | Framework Intrinsic | Framework Stereo |
|--------|-------|---------------------|------------------|
| Current limit (Hg EDM) | <2×10^-25 | 5×10^8 × too large | 5×10^7 × too large |

| SM prediction | ~10^-31–10^-33 | 10^15 × too large | 10^14 × too large |

Even with an extra α_em ≈ 1/137 suppression, the prediction remains above
the experimental bound. An α_em^2 suppression brings the stereographic case to
~6×10^-22 e·cm — still an order of magnitude above the indirect limit.

2.4 THE MISSING PHYSICS: CP VIOLATION

The framework contains NO CP-violating mechanism. The 2O self-dual/anti-self-
dual projection determines a SIGN (proton vs neutron EDM relative orientation),
but it does not explain why an EDM exists at all.

An EDM requires both P and T violation (or CP violation under CPT). The T-Knot
geometry is:
  • T-symmetric: A rotating 24-cell reversed in time is still a valid rotation.
  • P-symmetric: The 24-cell is centrally symmetric; inversion maps it to
    itself.

The computed ~10^-16 e·cm is not a prediction of a small CP-violating effect;
it is the geometric baseline of a classical rotating object, with no quantum
suppression mechanism identified.

2.5 ASSESSMENT

Status: REJECTED.

The EDM formula is excluded by experiment by many orders of magnitude. The
target ~10^-29 e·cm was chosen to match BNL sensitivity, not derived from
framework constants. No 10^-12 suppression factor exists in the 24-cell, Golay,
Leech, or E8 structures.

PART III: RC-39 — CANONICAL D: GREAT HEXAGON INVARIANT PLANE (FAILED)
================================================================================

3.1 PRE-REGISTRATION

Hypothesis: The great hexagon invariant plane — the natural rotation plane of
the 24-cell, fixed by its 6-fold cyclic symmetry — yields an intrinsic
α_knot consistent with the structurally motivated value 2 (the Hurwitz-to-axial
vertex ratio 16/8).

Falsification criterion: If intrinsic computation yields α_knot outside
[1.90, 2.2], the T-Knot mechanism is downgraded to CANDIDATE--PARAMETRIZED.

3.2 METHODOLOGY

The great hexagon invariant plane is spanned by:
  e1 = (1, 0, 0, 0) [axial]
  e2 = (0, 1, 1, 1)/√3 [Hurwitz direction]

This plane contains 6 vertices at radius r=1 (a great hexagon) and 18 vertices
at r=1/√3. The isoclinic rotation in this plane was analyzed for:
  • L_total: total orbit arc length over 720° (4π)
  • N: number of asynchronous near-approach local minima below edge length
  • w: mean minimum distance at crossings (passage width)
  • D: total arc length in "intersecting state" (distance < edge length)
  • α_knot = ((g_p − 2)/2) × (D/(N·w))

3.3 RESULTS

| Quantity | Computed | RC-34 Reported | Match |
|----------|----------|----------------|-------|
| L_total | 205.99 | 205.99 | ✓ |
| N | 390 | 390 | ✓ |
| w | 0.762 rad | 0.762 rad | ✓ |
| D | — | 171.5 | (algorithm-dependent) |

Using RC-34's reported D = 171.5:

  α_knot = ((g_p − 2)/2) × (D/(N·w))
         = 1.7946 × 0.5771
         = 1.036

3.4 KEY FINDINGS

(1) The great hexagon invariant plane — the most symmetry-derived rotation
    plane available — yields α_knot ≈ 1.04, far from the target 2.

(2) This is consistent with the RC-34 "generic rotation" result (1.03),
    confirming that the great hexagon plane was indeed the "generic" plane
    tested in RC-34.

(3) The v7.5 claimed identity D_axial/L_total = 11/32 is confirmed as a
    stereographic projection artifact. The intrinsic ratio is 0.833, not
    an integer ratio.

(4) α_knot is not an intrinsic invariant of the 24-cell's rotation geometry.
    It depends on:
      • Rotation plane choice (XY-plane: 2.24, great hexagon: 1.04)
      • Threshold definition for "intersecting state"
      • Whether stereographic or intrinsic metric is used

3.5 ASSESSMENT

Status: CANDIDATE--PARAMETRIZED.

Per the pre-registered falsification criterion, the T-Knot mechanism is
downgraded. No further T-Knot physical predictions are permitted until a
canonical rotation plane is derived from first principles (not selected for
its match to a target).

tensor product ring of 2O, the 33/17 coefficient is unexplained and the
neutron g-factor formula is downgraded to CANDIDATE--PARAMETRIZED.

4.2 METHODOLOGY

Step 1: Verify the 2O character table (8 irreps, 8 conjugacy classes).
Step 2: Compute all 36 tensor product decompositions via the standard formula:
  m_k = (1/|G|) Σ_g χ_i(g) χ_j(g) χ_k(g)
Step 3: Search for 33/17 or 17/33 in:
  • Dimension ratios
  • Tensor product multiplicities
  • Character values
  • Dimension sums/differences/products
  • Tensor product dimensions
  • Subgroup branching (2T, C8)

4.4 KEY FINDINGS

(1) The sign character (sgn) distinguishes 2D_spin (proton, even
    permutations) from 2D_spin' (neutron, odd permutations). This mechanism
    is CONFIRMED.

(2) The central element −1 ∈ 2O maps to −I in 2D_spin. The double cover

(3) The coefficient 33/17 = (3×11)/17 combines:
      • 3 = λ (Golay difference-set modulus)
      • 11 = √λ₁λ₁₂ (Gram eigenvalue product root)
      • 17 = 29−12 (Gram large eigenvalue trace component)
    These are COMBINATORIAL/ARITHMETIC constants, NOT representation-theoretic

(4) The neutron g-factor magnitude depends on a coefficient selected from
    Golay/Gram arithmetic, not derived from group theory.

4.5 ASSESSMENT

Status: CANDIDATE--PARAMETRIZED.

The neutron g-factor formula is downgraded. The sign is derived from 2O
representation theory. The magnitude is selected from combinatorial constants.
This is exactly the guardrail A.1a scenario: "searches over combinatorial
possibilities are guaranteed to produce close matches."

(5) No CP-violating mechanism exists in the framework. The proton EDM

(C) DOWNGRADE: If no new mechanism is found after additional research cycles,
