CORE HYPOTHESIS
================================================================================

The non-Clifford gate is not a symmetry of the crystal (Golay code / Leech
lattice). It is the geometric phase accumulated by LIGHT as it traverses
the crystal's orbit.

  - The crystal = Golay code C_24 (perfectly symmetric, Clifford gates only)
  - The shadows = Cocode F_2^24 / C (4096 cosets, NOT symmetric)
  - The light = superposition over all cocode cosets (everywhere and nowhere)
  - The orbit = engine action (z23, z11) on cocode cosets
  - The phase = Pancharatnam-Berry geometric phase accumulated along orbit

The light enters the crystal from OUTSIDE. It is not a codeword. It is a
superposition over all possible shadows. As the engine rotates the crystal
(ticks), the light sees different faces. When the light completes a closed
loop (23 ticks of z23, or 253 ticks of the full engine), it returns to its
starting shadow but with a phase.

If that phase is pi/8, the light has implemented a T-gate — a non-Clifford
operation.

COMPUTATIONAL PROTOCOL
================================================================================

Step 1: Define the Superpositioned Light State
-----------------------------------------------
The light state is an equal superposition over all 4096 cocode cosets:

  |Psi> = (1/sqrt(4096)) * sum_{s in cocode} |s> 

where |s> is a state in the syndrome subspace corresponding to cocode coset s.

In the stabilizer formalism, each syndrome subspace is a 2^12-dimensional
subspace of the full 2^24-dimensional Hilbert space. The cocode vectors
label these subspaces.

Step 2: Define the Engine's Action on the Cocode
--------------------------------------------------
The z23 automorphism is a permutation of the 24 physical qubits. It acts on
cocode cosets by permuting the syndrome bits. Since z23 preserves the Golay
code, it maps each syndrome subspace to another syndrome subspace.

Compute the permutation induced by z23 on the 4096 cocode cosets. Verify
that it has period 23.

Step 3: Evolve the Light State Along the Orbit
----------------------------------------------
Apply z23 repeatedly to |Psi>:

  |Psi_k> = z23^k |Psi>

After each step, project onto the cocode basis (measure the syndrome).
The relative phase between |Psi_k> and |Psi_0> is the geometric phase.

Step 4: Compute the Pancharatnam Phase
--------------------------------------
For a discrete orbit, the Pancharatnam phase is:

  gamma = arg<Psi_0 | Psi_n> - sum_{k=0}^{n-1} arg<Psi_k | Psi_{k+1}>

where n = 23 (one full z23 cycle).

The first term is the total phase. The second term is the dynamical phase.
The difference is the geometric (Berry) phase.

Step 5: Check for Non-Clifford Phase
------------------------------------
If gamma = pi/8 (mod 2*pi), the light has implemented a T-gate.
If gamma = pi/4 (mod 2*pi), the light has implemented an S-gate.
If gamma = 0, pi/2, pi, or 3*pi/2, the phase is Clifford.

HONEST PRE-ASSESSMENT
================================================================================

This cycle addresses the framework's oldest open question: where is the
non-Clifford gate? Past cycles (RC-75 through RC-77, RC-103 through RC-106)
have exhaustively tested symmetries of the crystal and found only Clifford
gates. The cocode dynamics hypothesis is the first test that looks OUTSIDE
the crystal — at the shadows cast by the crystal, not the crystal itself.

The risk is high: discrete permutations generally don't accumulate geometric
phases on symmetric superpositions. The Pancharatnam phase requires
continuous evolution or a non-trivial connection. The z23 orbit is discrete.

However, the cocode is not the code. The superpositioned light is not a
codeword. The engine's action on the cocode is not a symmetry — it's a
permutation of shadows. This asymmetry is the first genuinely new ingredient
in the framework's quantum information theory layer.

================================================================================
COMPUTATIONAL NOTES FOR EXECUTING AI
================================================================================

The executing AI should be aware of the following computational
considerations:

  1. The cocode has 4096 cosets. The full Hilbert space is 2^24-dimensional.
     The syndrome subspaces are 2^12-dimensional each. The superpositioned
     light state is a specific state in the 2^24-dimensional space, not a
     density matrix.

  2. The z23 action on cocode cosets is a permutation of 4096 elements.
     This can be computed efficiently by tracking the action on syndrome
     bits, not by full Hilbert space simulation.

  3. The Pancharatnam phase computation requires inner products between
     states at different ticks. For a permutation of orthogonal subspaces,
     these inner products are zero unless the permutation maps a subspace
     to itself. This is the key computational challenge.

  4. If the naive computation gives zero overlap (orthogonal subspaces),
     the executing AI should consider:
      a. Using coherent states peaked at cocode vectors (not equal superposition)
      b. Defining a connection (parallel transport rule) between subspaces
      c. Computing the Berry phase for a continuous interpolation of the
         engine's action

  5. The Gram matrix eigenvalues (29 +/- 12*sqrt(5)) may provide a natural
     metric for defining coherent states and connections. The executing AI
     should consider whether the QR-basis geometry can be used to define
     a natural connection on the cocode bundle.
