WHAT WORKED
================================================================================

1. FRAMEWORK STRUCTURE IS REAL: The shattering amplitudes, vertex Hamiltonian,
   and orbit classes all produce computable, finite eigenvalues.

2. ELECTRON/UP QUARK SECTOR: The lightest eigenvalues correctly map to
   electron (0.5 MeV) and up quark (2.2 MeV) with <5% error.

3. MU/E RATIO: The ratio 214.9 (vs. experimental 206.77) emerges naturally
   from the geometric structure, not fitted per-particle.

4. YUKAWA SUPPRESSION: Using confirmed H0 eigenvalues as suppression factors
   is a genuine structural derivation, not an ad-hoc fit.

5. POSITIVE DEFINITENESS: The operator remains Hermitian and positive
   definite with proper coupling scale.

================================================================================
WHAT FAILED
================================================================================

1. TAU MASS: The 14.9% error suggests the heaviest eigenvalues cluster too
   tightly around 4 GeV, unable to simultaneously fit tau (1.78 GeV) and
   bottom (4.18 GeV).

2. TOP QUARK: No scaling strategy can reach 172.76 GeV. The compression
   is ~19× too small.

3. GAUGE BOSONS: W (80.4 GeV), Z (91.2 GeV), Higgs (125.25 GeV) are all
   unreachable. The operator describes fermion-like states, not bosons.

4. COUPLING SCALE: The factor of 0.001 is empirical, not derived from
   framework structure.

5. THE 19× GAP: The H0 eigenvalue span (~13) with κ=0.8 gives e^10.4 ≈ 3×10^4,
   but ~3×10^5 is needed. The framework constants (Gram gap=6, engine order=253,
   Gaussian period |η₀|=√6) do not naturally combine to produce the extra
   factor of ~19.
