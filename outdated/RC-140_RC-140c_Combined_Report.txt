## 7.3 Fundamental Obstacle

**Theorem (empirical):** No proper subspace of ℝ²⁴ spanned by a subset of
deep holes is preserved by both M₁₁ and U_θ.

Proof sketch:
  • Deep holes have Gram matrix G with Gᵢᵢ = 6, Gᵢⱼ = 5 (i≠j)
  • Any subset S ⊂ {0,...,23} spans a subspace with large overlap (~90%)
    with its complement
  • U_θ rotates in the (0,23) plane, which has non-zero projection onto all
    deep holes
  • Therefore U_θ maps any such subspace outside itself

The only subspaces preserved by both operators would require:
  1. Orthogonal basis vectors (not deep holes)
  2. Common eigenvectors of M₁₁ and U_θ

But M₁₁ and U_θ do not commute, so they have no common eigenbasis.

dynamics.

# 12. HONEST LIMITATIONS

1. The 7-cycle decomposition was computed for exact deep holes under the full
tick F. For general states (superpositions, noisy states), the dynamics may
have a different effective cycle structure.

2. The subspace overlap threshold of 0.99 is arbitrary. Sensitivity analysis
should be performed with thresholds 0.95, 0.90, 0.80.

3. The logical qubit overlap |⟨0_L|1_L⟩| = 0.941 is not orthogonal. The sections
are distinguishable but not maximally separated. Better section designs may exist.

4. The framework uses real vectors, not complex amplitudes. The "phase" is a
real angle in the (0,23) plane, not a quantum phase. The logical "qubit" may
not have standard quantum properties (superposition, entanglement).

5. The test grid for θ is discrete. A continuous test might reveal additional
failure modes at specific angles.

6. The geometric meaning of the 7 cycles remains unknown. They do not correspond
cleanly to orbit classes, axes, or known geometric structures.

7. No discrete base (7-point, 13-point, 2-point, or 5-point) supports a fiber
bundle. The deep hole non-orthogonality (inner product 5/6) is a fundamental
