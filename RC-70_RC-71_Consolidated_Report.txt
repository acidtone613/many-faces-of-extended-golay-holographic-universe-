1.5 W(F4) EQUIVARIANCE (STRUCTURAL ARGUMENT)
--------------------------------------------

The barycentric subdivision is defined purely by the incidence structure
of the 24-cell. The Dirac operator D = d + d† is constructed from the
boundary operators d1, d2, d3. Any combinatorial automorphism of the
24-cell that preserves simplex incidence must commute with D.

The 24-cell's automorphism group is W(F4) (order 1,152).

The observed multiplicities (4, 6, 8, 9, 12, 16, 18, 25, 27, 32, 38, 44, 95)
are not pure W(F4) irrep dimensions. This is expected: the Hodge Laplacian
couples the tensor-product factors of the barycentric chain spaces
(C¹ ≅ (C⁰_vertices ⊗ C¹_edges) ⊕ ...), so eigenvalues need not organize
into pure irrep dimensions. This is the tensor product obstruction noted
in RC-70 §5.3.

Explicit construction of all 1,152 group elements was skipped because the
structural argument is sufficient: the operator is symmetry-preserving by
construction, yet this symmetry does NOT force the required 2D degeneracy.
