2.4 THE 22-DIMENSIONAL REAL REPRESENTATION
================================================================================

The 22-dimensional real space is the orthogonal complement of the uniform
mode in the 23-dimensional permutation space on positions 0..22.

Basis construction:
    Let v_uniform = (1, 1, ..., 1)/√23  (the uniform vector in R^23)
    Let P_uniform = v_uniform v_uniform^T  (projector onto uniform mode)
    Let P_ortho = I_23 - P_uniform  (projector onto 22-dim subspace)

The 22-dim subspace is spanned by the columns of P_ortho. An orthonormal
basis is obtained via QR decomposition or SVD of P_ortho.

In this subspace:
    • P23 acts as a 22×22 orthogonal matrix (permutation minus uniform)
    • P11 acts as a 22×22 orthogonal matrix (block-diagonal on A and B)

    α = 3.0 (Gram gap/2) produces the most structured spectrum.

    V = |A⟩⟨B| + |B⟩⟨A|

where |A⟩ and |B⟩ are the normalized characteristic vectors of Orbit A
and Orbit B, projected onto the 22-dim subspace and orthonormalized:

    v_A = Σ_{i∈Orbit A} e_i  (characteristic vector of A)
    v_B = Σ_{i∈Orbit B} e_i  (characteristic vector of B)

    v_A^⊥ = v_A - (v_A · v_uniform) v_uniform  (orthogonalize to uniform)
    v_B^⊥ = v_B - (v_B · v_uniform) v_uniform

    |A⟩ = v_A^⊥ / ||v_A^⊥||
    |B⟩ = v_B^⊥ / ||v_B^⊥||

V is symmetric, rank-2, and swaps the A and B subspaces.

The perturbed operator:
    H(ε) = H_0 + εV

where ε is the perturbation scale.
