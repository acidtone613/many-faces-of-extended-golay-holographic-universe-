This document reports the execution of Research Cycles RC-55, RC-56, and
RC-57, conducted under the Standing Orders of the v7.9 framework.

The cycles tested the simplicial Dirac operator (RC-54's lead mechanism)
against symmetry-derived mode selection, cross-sector falsification, and
an external structural candidate (I Ching).

Key outcomes:
  • RC-55: FAILED — 2O symmetry does not force modes 173/174 in the
    canonical barycentric triangulation. The triangulation breaks symmetry.
  • RC-56: FAILED — Dirac operator is nucleon-sector-limited. No spectral
    match for Λ–Σ⁰; pion match (0.092%) is coincidental and physically
    mismatched.
  • RC-57: REJECTED — I Ching contains no structural forcing for 727/726
    or any framework constant beyond trivial numerical coincidences.

RC-55 — MODE SELECTION PRINCIPLE: 2O SYMMETRY ON THE BARYCENTRIC 24-CELL
================================================================================

PRE-REGISTRATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Hypothesis: Eigenmodes 173 and 174 of the Dirac operator on the barycentrically
subdivided 24-cell are forced into the 2D spin representations of 2O by the
symmetry group's action on the simplicial complex.

Constraint: The triangulation is canonical (lexicographic diagonal choice).
No retuning of the operator is permitted.

Falsification criterion (stated before computation):
  • CANDIDATE: Modes 173/174 (or their eigenspinor subspaces) fall in the
    2D spin irreps of 2O, and this is forced by spectral gap or symmetry.
  • CANDIDATE–PARAMETRIZED: Modes 173/174 are simple (multiplicity 1) and
    carry 1D irreps only; no 2D spin structure is accessible.
  • REJECTED: The symmetry group action does not commute with the Dirac
    operator, or the eigenspaces are not representation-theoretically forced.

METHODOLOGY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Step 1: Construct the 24-cell geometry.
  • Vertices: 24 permutations of (±1, ±1, 0, 0) — the D4 root vectors.
  • Edges: 96 pairs at distance √2.
  • Faces: 96 triangular 3-cliques.
  • Cells: 24 regular octahedra.
  • Verification: edge incidence (3 cells/edge), face incidence (2 cells/face),
    vertex incidence (6 cells/vertex). ✓

Step 2: Barycentric triangulation of each octahedral cell.
  • Each octahedron has 3 internal diagonals (opposite vertices at distance 2).
  • Choose one diagonal per octahedron (first in lexicographic order).
  • The 4 equator vertices form a square; subdivide with one diagonal into
    2 triangles.
  • Form 4 tetrahedra: (apex₁, tri₁), (apex₁, tri₂), (apex₂, tri₁), (apex₂, tri₂).
  • Result: 96 tetrahedra, 192 triangular faces, 120 edges, 24 vertices.
  • Verification: each face in exactly 2 tetrahedra (closed 3-manifold).
    Euler characteristic = 0. ✓

Step 3: Build chain complex and boundary operators.
  • C₀: 24 vertices, C₁: 120 edges, C₂: 192 faces, C₃: 96 tetrahedra.
  • Boundary operators d₁, d₂, d₃ with consistent orientation.
  • Verification: d₁·d₂ = 0, d₂·d₃ = 0 (to machine precision). ✓

Step 4: Compute Dirac operator.
  • D = d + d* (full Dirac operator on C₀ ⊕ C₁ ⊕ C₂ ⊕ C₃, 432×432).
  • Verification: D = Dᵀ (self-adjoint), D² = block-diag(L₀, L₁, L₂, L₃). ✓

Step 5: Compute spectrum.
  • All eigenvalues computed densely.
  • Betti numbers from zero-mode count: [1, 0, 0, 1] — matches S³. ✓

Step 6: Construct symmetry group action.
  • Search for signed permutations of R⁴ that preserve the triangulation.
  • Build induced representation on the chain complex (432×432 matrices).
  • Test commutation with D.

RESULTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Dirac spectrum (confirmed from RC-54):
  • Positive eigenvalues: 215
  • Best nucleon ratio match: μ₁₇₄/μ₁₇₃ = 1.001380784 vs. 727/726 = 1.001377410
  • Error: 0.0003% — the most precise numerical match in framework history.

Symmetry analysis:
  • Signed permutations preserving triangulation: 2 found.
  • Symmetries commuting with D: 1 (the identity only).
  • The lexicographic diagonal choice breaks all non-trivial symmetry.

Eigenspace structure:
  • Modes 173 and 174 in the positive spectrum are simple (multiplicity 1).
  • A 1D eigenspace can only carry a 1-dimensional irrep of any symmetry group.
  • The 2D spin representations of 2O (dimensions {1,1,2,2,3,3,4,4}) are
    inaccessible to simple eigenmodes.

KEY FINDINGS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. The canonical barycentric triangulation (lexicographic diagonal choice)
   is symmetry-breaking. Only the identity element commutes with D.

2. Eigenmodes 173 and 174 are simple and carry no 2O spin structure in this
   triangulation.

3. The mode selection is NOT forced by 2O symmetry. The indices are selected,
   not derived — the same methodological position as the T-Knot's δ = π/284.

GUARDRAIL CHECK (A.1a)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The match μ₁₇₄/μ₁₇₃ = 1.001380784 is numerically excellent but structurally
unexplained. No group-theoretic forcing selects these modes. This is precisely
the guardrail A.1a scenario: a selected combination, not a derived one.

ASSESSMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Status: FAILED (per pre-registered criterion).

The mode selection principle is not achieved in the canonical triangulation.
The Dirac operator remains CANDIDATE–PARAMETRIZED with one unexplained parameter
(the mode indices).

RC-56 — CROSS-SECTOR DIRAC TEST
================================================================================

PRE-REGISTRATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Hypothesis: The same Dirac spectrum (no retuning) reproduces a second
independent hadronic mass ratio.

Targets:
  • Λ–Σ⁰ mass ratio = m_Σ⁰/m_Λ = 1192.642/1115.683 = 1.068979
  • Charged–neutral pion ratio = m_π⁺/m_π⁰ = 139.57018/134.9766 = 1.034032
  • Nucleon ratio (control) = m_n/m_p = 727/726 = 1.001377

Falsification criterion (stated before computation):
  • CANDIDATE: A ratio in the target band matches within ±10% AND the mode
    indices are adjacent (structural signal).
  • CANDIDATE–PARAMETRIZED: A ratio matches but indices are not adjacent or
    the physical mechanism is mismatched.
  • REJECTED: No ratio in the target band falls within ±10%.

METHODOLOGY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Use the pre-computed Dirac spectrum from RC-54/RC-55 (215 positive eigenvalues).
2. Search all adjacent eigenvalue ratios in bands:
   • [1.001, 1.002] for nucleon
   • [1.03, 1.04] for pion
   • [1.06, 1.08] for Λ–Σ⁰
3. For each match, record: ratio, error vs target, mode indices, adjacency.
4. Physical mechanism check: is the target a strong-interaction effect
   (appropriate for the Dirac operator) or electromagnetic?

RESULTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

| Sector          | Target     | Band        | Matches | Best Error | Verdict |
|-----------------|------------|-------------|---------|------------|---------|
| Nucleon (control) | 1.001377 | [1.001, 1.002] | 29 adjacent | 0.0003% | Confirmed |
| Pion (π⁺/π⁰)    | 1.034032   | [1.03, 1.04] | 3       | 0.092%     | Curiosity |
| Λ–Σ⁰            | 1.068979   | [1.06, 1.08] | **0**   | —          | Failed |

Detailed findings:

Nucleon sector (control):
  • Best match: indices 173/174, ratio 1.001380784, error 0.0003%.
  • 29 adjacent ratios in [1.001, 1.002]; best is 20× better than any non-adjacent.
  • Adjacency is a real structural feature of the spectrum.

Pion sector:
  • Best match: indices 8/9, ratio 1.034986, error 0.092%.
  • Indices are low in the spectrum (generic low-lying modes, not special).
  • Physical mechanism mismatch: pion splitting is electromagnetic
    (Coulomb energy + m_u − m_d), not strong-interaction.
  • Only 3 ratios in [1.03, 1.04]; statistical significance low.

Λ–Σ⁰ sector:
  • **Zero ratios** in [1.06, 1.08].
  • Complete absence, not a near-miss.
  • Same failure mode as T-Knot standing wave (RC-51).

KEY FINDINGS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. The Dirac operator is nucleon-sector-limited. It reproduces the nucleon
   mass ratio with unprecedented precision but has no spectral structure in
   the strange-baryon regime.

2. The pion "match" is coincidental: low-lying modes, electromagnetic target,
   poor statistical significance.

3. The Dirac operator has passed the same bar as the T-Knot (nucleon ratio)
   and failed the same cross-sector test (Λ–Σ⁰).

ASSESSMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Status: FAILED (per pre-registered criterion for cross-sector validation).

The Dirac operator is not a universal engine. It is a nucleon-sector engine
with one parameter (mode selection).

RC-57 — I CHING STRUCTURAL CORRESPONDENCE TEST
================================================================================

PRE-REGISTRATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Hypothesis: The I Ching's combinatorial structure (64 hexagrams, 32 pairs,
8 trigrams, 6 lines) contains structural forcing for framework targets,
including the nucleon mass ratio 727/726 or the mode selection principle.

Falsification criterion (stated before computation):
  • CANDIDATE: A theorem or spectral property of the I Ching forces a
    framework target (ratio, constant, or mode index) without post-hoc
    selection.
  • EXPLORATORY: Numerical coincidences exist but no theorem forces them.
  • REJECTED: No non-trivial structural correspondence exists; all matches
    are trivial (small integers coinciding) or numerological.

METHODOLOGY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Step 1: Construct I Ching mathematical skeleton.
  • 64 hexagrams as 6-bit binary strings (Fu Xi / Shao Yong ordering).
  • 8 trigrams as 3-bit strings.
  • King Wen sequence as verified permutation of 0–63.

Step 2: Verify King Wen sequence.
  • Source: TCL I Ching package (academic reference).
  • 64 unique values, range 0–63, no missing. ✓

Step 3: Transition analysis.
  • Compute Hamming distances between adjacent hexagrams in King Wen sequence.
  • Count even vs odd line changes.

Step 4: Pair analysis.
  • King Wen pairs: (1,2), (3,4), ..., (63,64).
  • Check if pairs are related by 180° rotation or bitwise complement.

Step 5: Spectral analysis.
  • Build transition matrix for King Wen sequence dynamics.
  • Compute eigenvalues.
  • Search for framework targets in spectrum.

Step 6: Hypercube graph Laplacian.
  • Hexagrams are vertices of Q₆ (6-dimensional hypercube).
  • Adjacency: Hamming distance 1.
  • Compute Laplacian spectrum (known analytically: eigenvalues 2k with
    multiplicities C(6,k) for k=0,...,6).
  • Search for framework targets in eigenvalue ratios.

Step 7: Structural number comparison.
  • Compare I Ching numbers {2,4,6,8,16,32,64,192,384} with framework
    constants {24,48,96,240,3,17,121,726,727}.
  • Check exact matches, ratio matches, and constructibility.

RESULTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
King Wen transition analysis:
  • Adjacent Hamming distances: {1: 2, 2: 20, 3: 13, 4: 19, 6: 9}
  • Even changes: 48, Odd changes: 15
  • Even:odd ratio = 3.20:1 (vs. random expectation 1:1, deviation ~16.5σ)

King Wen pair analysis:
  • 28 pairs related by 180° rotation (bitwise reversal).
  • 4 pairs related by bitwise complement.
  • 0 pairs with other relationships.

Hypercube Q₆ Laplacian spectrum (verified):
  • Eigenvalues: 0×1, 2×6, 4×15, 6×20, 8×15, 10×6, 12×1
  • Total: 64 eigenvalues. ✓

Framework target search in Q₆ spectrum:
  • Nucleon (727/726 = 1.001377): Best ratio = 1.0, error 0.1376%.
    (This is not 1.001377; it is ratio 1.0, which is trivial.)
  • Pion (1.034032): Best ratio = 1.0, error 3.29%.
  • Λ–Σ⁰ (1.068979): Best ratio = 1.0, error 6.45%.

Structural number comparison:
  • Exact matches: 6=6 (lines/Coxeter D4), 2=2 (binary/spinor double cover).
    These are trivial small-integer coincidences.
  • 727 and 726: Cannot be constructed from I Ching numbers {2,6,8,32,64,192,384}.
  • Prime 11 (in 726 = 2×3×11²): Absent from all I Ching combinatorics.
  • Prime 727: Prime, absent from I Ching.

Cross-ratio matching:
  • Best match: pairs/trigrams = 32/8 = 4 equals 24-cell edges/vertices = 96/24 = 4.
    Error: 0.0000%.
    Assessment: Numerical coincidence. No structural map from King Wen pairs
    to 24-cell edge geometry.

KEY FINDINGS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. The I Ching is a 6-dimensional hypercube (Q₆) with a distinguished
   permutation (King Wen sequence). Its mathematics is entirely within
   the binary group Z₂⁶.

2. The "3:1 ratio" of even:odd line changes is a real structural feature
   of the King Wen permutation (~16.5σ from random). It is not derived
   from the Golay (11,6,3)-difference set. These are different "3"s.

3. The I Ching contains no framework target numbers (11, 24, 48, 96, 121,
   240, 726, 727) in its combinatorics.

4. The Q₆ Laplacian spectrum {0,2,4,6,8,10,12} cannot produce 727/726.
   The eigenvalues are too coarse (spacing = 2).

5. All exact numerical matches (6=6, 2=2, 4=4) are trivial small-integer
   coincidences that appear throughout mathematics.

GUARDRAIL CHECK (A.1a)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The I Ching mapping uses:
  • Numbers from an external system (64, 32, 8, 6)
  • Arithmetic operations (division, ratio)
  • Post-hoc physical correspondence (hexagrams = faces, pairs = 2O, etc.)

None of these steps is forced by the 24-cell geometry or any framework theorem.
The mapping is selected, not derived. This is precisely the guardrail A.1a
scenario.

ASSESSMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Status: REJECTED.

The I Ching is a beautiful binary combinatorial system with no structural
forcing for any framework target. The mapping remains INTERPRETIVE.

FINAL SYNTHESIS: RC-55 THROUGH RC-57
================================================================================

WHAT HAS BEEN ACHIEVED
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. RC-55 rigorously tested the mode selection hypothesis. The canonical
   barycentric triangulation breaks 2O symmetry, and modes 173/174 are
   simple (1D) eigenmodes with no representation-theoretic forcing.

2. RC-56 honestly evaluated the Dirac operator against cross-sector targets.
   The operator is nucleon-sector-limited, matching the T-Knot's scope but
   with superior precision.

3. RC-57 computationally audited the I Ching correspondence. No structural
   forcing was found. The mapping is interpretive, not derived.

WHAT REMAINS OPEN
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Mode selection principle. A symmetry-preserving triangulation (true
   barycentric subdivision with edge midpoints, face centroids, cell centroids)
   could preserve 2O symmetry and force eigenspaces into irreps. This would
   grow the Dirac operator from 432×432 to ~2000×2000.

2. The one-parameter framing. If mode selection is accepted as permanent,
   the framework's claim becomes: "1-parameter nucleon-sector engine" — a
   genuine reduction from 19 Standard Model parameters to 1, but not a
   zero-parameter derivation.

3. Genuinely unknown prediction. The framework has still never predicted
   an unmeasured quantity.

THE BOUNDARY CONDITION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Current state: (B) STABLE — with narrowing.

The framework has:
• A confirmed mathematical skeleton (unchanged).
• One lead dynamic mechanism (Dirac operator) with one parameter (mode indices).
• No cross-sector validation.
• No zero-parameter derivation.

The Dirac operator is the most promising object because:
1. It is mathematically canonical (no arbitrary choices beyond standard
   triangulation).
2. It respects the confirmed 24-cell geometry.
3. It yields the most precise numerical match to date (0.0003%).
4. It naturally carries spinor structure.

The critical missing piece remains the MODE SELECTION PRINCIPLE.
