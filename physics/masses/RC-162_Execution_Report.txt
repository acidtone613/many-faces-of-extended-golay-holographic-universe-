6.1 What Works
---------------

The Gram + H₀ suppression successfully predicts:
  • Tau lepton mass to <1% accuracy
  • Muon/electron ratio to 5% accuracy
  • Charm quark mass to 8% accuracy
  • Up quark mass to 9% accuracy

The intermediate mass hierarchy (e, μ, τ, u, c) emerges naturally from
the combined suppression structure.

6.2 What Fails
---------------

  • Top quark: Under-predicted by 98.4% — compression insufficient
  • Bottom quark: Under-predicted by 32% — at the edge of acceptability
  • Only 7 of 15 eigenvalues map cleanly to known fermions

The fundamental issue: the confirmed H₀ eigenvalues span [-6.5, +6.6],
so exp(κ·E) for negative E gives suppression < 1, while positive E
 gives enhancement. The total dynamic range is ~10⁴, far short of the
~3.4×10⁵ needed for the full SM spectrum.

6.3 The Eigenvalue Discrepancy
-------------------------------

The RC-162 preregistration claimed H₀ eigenvalues [0.043, 0.120, ..., 3.848]
that would have produced a much larger compression (all positive, spanning
0.04 to 3.85). However, these values:

  1. Do not match the confirmed RC-83 H₀ construction
  2. Do not match the unified Hamiltonian H_unified
  3. Do not match any confirmed framework operator

The first value (0.043478 = 1/23) matches the S-involution's near-zero
eigenvalue, but the remaining 10 values have no confirmed source in the
framework.
