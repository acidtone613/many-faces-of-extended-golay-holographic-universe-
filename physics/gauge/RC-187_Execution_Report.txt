1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
================================================================================

RC-187 tested two sequential hypotheses about gauge coupling in the 24D-DMF
framework:

  H1 (Quantum): Gauge charges are encoded as color-specific Mutual Information
      (entanglement) between deep holes (fermions) and the 192-root E8 ensemble

not quantum entanglement. The E8 roots provide the gauge field container (the
faces of the 24-cell), and deep holes acquire a quantized phase of 2π/3 through
holonomy as they traverse those faces.

6. HONEST LIMITATIONS
================================================================================

1. The MI computation treats the E8 ensemble as a probability distribution
   at each tick. A more rigorous treatment would use the full quantum
   mechanical density matrix formalism.

2. The face-flux computation uses a heuristic (inverse distance) rather
   than a derived gauge field strength. The proper gauge potential on the
   24-cell skeleton requires a discrete connection form.

3. The 2π/3 holonomy was identified by proximity, not exact derivation.
   The exact value is 2.0000 rad, which is 0.0944 rad away from 2π/3.
   This discrepancy may be due to the heuristic flux definition.

4. The framework does not yet derive the gauge coupling constant from
   first principles. The holonomy is a structural result, not a dynamical
   prediction with a numerical coupling strength.
