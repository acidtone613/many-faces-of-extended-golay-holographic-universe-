10. HONEST LIMITATIONS
================================================================================

1. The arc-length integration for H3 assumes linear interpolation in 24D and
   projection to S². The true continuous dynamics may have curvature that is
   not captured by this proxy.

2. The threshold ε = 0.3 was chosen as approximately half the typical deep hole
   separation. The sensitivity sweep confirms robustness, but the choice remains
   somewhat arbitrary.

3. H3 passes, but this does not automatically validate the RC-132 H3 failure.
   The split ratio analysis concerns a different question (discretization of
   non-degenerate orbits) than the speed-excess universality tested in RC-132.

4. The continuous trajectory uses floating-point arithmetic. While round-off is
   negligible for the fixed-point analysis (δ = 0 exactly), it may affect the
   split ratio integration at the 1e-12 level.

5. The post-hoc nature of this test (motivated by RC-132 failures) creates
   selection bias. However, the pre-registered protocol binds the results.
