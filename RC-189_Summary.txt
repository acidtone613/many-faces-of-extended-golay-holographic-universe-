4. INTERPRETATION
================================================================================

4.1 What the 4-Fold Degeneracy Means
-------------------------------------
The 4 equal eigenvalues (λ = 0.5) in the 24D covariance matrix indicate that
the 16 broken chiral roots span a 4-dimensional subspace of the 24D space with
ISOTROPIC variance. This is a genuine geometric property:

  • The 4D subspace corresponds to the quaternion fiber of the Hopf fibration.
  • The isotropy indicates the Floquet evolution preserves a rotational symmetry.
  • The 4 directions are geometrically equivalent at Tick 3.

This is NOT the electroweak gauge boson spectrum, which requires:
  • 3 massive modes with DIFFERENT masses (W+ = W- ≠ Z0)
  • 1 exactly massless mode (photon)

4.2 Why the 3+1 Structure Does Not Emerge
-------------------------------------------
The covariance matrix measures the QUADRATIC spread of the field configuration.
It captures the "Higgs vacuum manifold" geometry, not the gauge boson masses.

In the Standard Model:
  • The Higgs field VEV sets the SCALE of symmetry breaking (v ≈ 246 GeV).
  • The gauge boson masses come from the COUPLING of the gauge fields to the Higgs.
  • m_W = gv/2, m_Z = v√(g²+g'²)/2, m_γ = 0.

The 16 chiral roots alone do NOT contain the gauge coupling information.
They are the Higgs field configuration, not the gauge boson spectrum.

4.3 Possible Revisions to the Hypothesis
-----------------------------------------
Revision A: The 16 roots are the Higgs field. The gauge boson masses require
            computing the covariant derivative D_μφ = ∂_μφ + igA_μφ on the
            24-cell skeleton, where A_μ is the gauge connection.

Revision B: The 3+1 splitting might emerge from the INTERACTION between the
            16 chiral roots and the other 224 E8 roots (the gauge field
            candidates from the D4 blocks and other sectors).

Revision C: The mass hierarchy requires a QUARTIC term in the Higgs potential,

            covariance matrix.

Revision D: The 4-fold degeneracy IS the unbroken symmetry at Tick 3. The
            splitting into 3+1 might occur at a LATER tick (Tick 4+), after
            additional Floquet evolution breaks the residual symmetry.
